Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India


A. P. J. Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015),was a career scientist turned statesman, Kalam was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He was proudly called as the " The Missile Man of India " for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.Thus, he was honored to become the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."
- Abdul Kalam

Profile

Awards :

"If you fail, never give up because FAIL means "First Attempt In Learning".
- Abdul Kalam

Kalam started his career as a scientist at Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).Later he was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980.

The so called People's President,Kalam was not only a great scientist but also a great human who still amaze people by his simplicity and wisdom. He is an inspiration for many youngsters in India and always dreamed that one day India will became a "Super Power Nation". Thus, his indispensable qualities earned him the Bharat Ratna, the highest civilian award of the Republic of India.

“All Birds find shelter during a rain. But Eagle avoids rain by flying above the Clouds.”
- Abdul Kalam

Post presidency, he opted to pursue his dream career as a teacher. Unfortunately, he died on 27 July 2017 due to cardiac arrest while delivering lecture at the India Institute of Management,Shillong. It came as a big blow to the Nation.

As a tribute, a memorial ( Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam National Memorial ) was built in memory of him at Rameswaram, in the state of Tamil Nadu, India.
